Welcome

Hello, my name is Simon Ladouce. I am a postdoctoral researcher in cognitive neuroscience at ISAE-SUPAERO
My research aims to gain insight about how humans think and act in real-life contexts
For this I use brain and body imaging methods such as EEG, fNIRS, Eye-tracking, motion tracking and more
I currently work on the development of neurotechnologies to support pilots during flight operations

Research Projects

Improving user experience of BCI

Exploring different approaches to make Brain-Computer Interfaces less intrusive and more comfortable from an user experience perspective.

Brain dynamics during real-world activities

Capturing neural markers of cognitive functions during natural behaviours in naturalistic settings using portable brain and body imaging technologies.

Neural correlates of locomotion and gait adaptation

In collaboration with Magda Mustile.
Investigation of gait-related neural dynamics, obstacle negociation and movement initiation in healthy subjects and patients with Parkinson's disease.
